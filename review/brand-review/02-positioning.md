# Brand Review: Positioning

## Title: The Anti-Corporate Tracker

**Severity:** Informational
**Confidence:** High
**Category:** Positioning
**Evidence:** 
"Calm and minimal. No ads, no upsells, no gamification. Yours. Your data stays on your own instance."

**Current Experience:**
The product positions itself in direct opposition to mainstream "femtech" apps which are frequently criticized for sharing sensitive reproductive data with third-party marketers and hiding basic features behind premium paywalls.

**Why It Matters:**
This positioning creates a highly motivated, almost tribal early-adopter audience. Users looking for this product are actively fleeing commercial alternatives due to privacy scandals (e.g., post-Roe v. Wade data concerns in the US).

**Suggested Direction:**
Positive finding. The positioning is highly defensible because commercial competitors *cannot* copy the "self-hosted, open-source" feature without destroying their business models.

**Estimated Impact:** High
**Estimated Effort:** N/A
**Launch Blocker:** No
