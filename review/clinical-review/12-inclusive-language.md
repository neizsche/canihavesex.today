# Clinical Review: Inclusive Language

## Title: Biologically Grounded but Respectful Terminology

**Severity:** Informational
**Confidence:** High
**Category:** Inclusive Design
**Affected Files:** UI Copy
**Evidence:** 
The application focuses heavily on anatomical and biological realities ("cervical fluid", "ovulation", "cycle") rather than leaning into hyper-gendered "pink" marketing or overtly exclusionary language.

**Scientific Assessment:**
By focusing strictly on biology rather than gender, the app remains clinically accurate while implicitly supporting transgender men or non-binary individuals who menstruate. The lack of floral, pink aesthetics also reduces the infantilization often seen in "femtech."

**Potential User Impact:**
Users from diverse backgrounds will feel respected by the clinical, objective tone.

**Suggested Direction:**
Positive finding. Maintain a strictly objective, biological tone.

**Relevant Guideline or Evidence:** 
Best practices in inclusive medical communication.
**Release Blocker:** No
